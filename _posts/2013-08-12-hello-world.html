Hello World

I wanted my first post to be like a typical "Hello World" program people do.  The point isn't to solve a problem but to learn about a given technology.  People have gotten more creative about the "Hello World" program - e.g. the TODO app for MV* frameworks.

Why Blog

I am terrible at writing.  I did not like taking English writing courses growing up.  I enjoyed diagramming sentences more than writing sentences.  I am starting to blog because I am hoping that it will benefit me.  Kind of a selfish reason but oh well.  Nathan Marz had a good blog post about blogging.  The reasons on his post are some of the reasons why I am starting to blog.  I do hope that at least one of my blog posts will benefit someone, but that is more of a secondary reason.

I've noticed over the past few months that as I read a topic, I get to a certain level of understanding.  If I talk with others and try to explain what I read to them, I am able to get to a deeper level of understanding because they might ask questions I hadn't thought of and I have to revisit the topic.  I have begun writing notes on Google Docs and feel that by writing down my thoughts on a given topic I am able to get to an even deeper level of understanding.  These three things - reading, discussing, and writing - all fit under one piece: learning the theoretical.  The other piece - which is just as important - is the doing: going through the tutorial of a given technology, try to solve a known (to you) problem with that technology, contributing back to that technology, etc.

My hope is to get better at both.  So, blogging is my venture into reading, discussing, and finally writing about what I learn.  I will try as much as possible to cite my references.  In some cases, it will be Wikipedia.

"Wikipedia is the best thing ever. Anyone in the world can write anything they want, so you know you are getting the best possible information." - Michael Scott

Filling the gaps

I find sometimes that as I learn something and implement what I have learned, there are times when I take a step back and realize I have some missing gaps in my knowledge.  Then, I will try to fill in those missing gaps.  My first set of blogs will be around some gaps that I have recently filled: data structures and algorithms.  I feel that these are the essential building blocks - i.e. tools - for any software engineer.  I want to have the most amount of tools for my craft, so I will be able to solve problems in the best possible manner given its inputs and outputs instead of having the mindset of having my hammer and everything looks like a nail to me.

In conclusion...

My wife is a teacher and she hates it when kids use 'In conclusion' to finish their writing, so this is for her :P.  I do hope that people will read this and get something out of it.  I know I will get something out of this by getting better at something I am not good at - writing - and being able to more clearly consolidate and explain my ideas.  If you have any feedback, please be constructive about it.  Thanks and enjoy!
